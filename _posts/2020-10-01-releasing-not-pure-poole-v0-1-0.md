MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are small noncoding RNAs that control gene expression by binding to the 3′ untranslated regions of target messenger RNAs and inhibiting(抑制) their translation into protein.MiRNAs are important for the development and function of multiple cell types, including T cells.For example, the miRNA miR-146a is required for homeostasis of regulatory T cells and the suppression of inflammation, whereas miR-155 is necessary for the differentiation of T cells and the regulation of immune responses.

Type 2 helper T (Th2) cells are important for immune responses against infections and allergens. These cells produce characteristic cytokines(细胞因子), such as interleukin-4 (IL-4), IL-5, and IL-13, which have critical roles in promoting immunity in response to antigen stimulation.Although several transcription factors(转录因子) have been implicated in the differentiation and function of Th2 cells,the role of miRNAs in regulating Th2 responses is poorly understood. MiR-21 has been reported to support the activity of IL-4 in mouse Th2 cells,but the function of other miRNAs in human Th2 cells has not been clearly investigated in depth.

Therefore,we carried out a study.We sorted CD4+ T cells from human lung tissue and differentiated(使分化) them into Th2 cells with IL-4 and IL-13. These cells produced IL-4, IL-5, and IL-13 and expressed some Th2-specific transcription factors.And then we analyzed miRNA expression in these cells compared with nonpolarized (非极化)(Thp) CD4+ T cells by using a technique.We found that of the miRNAs that were differentially expressed in Th2 cells versus Thp cells, miRNA-205 was the most striking, with an expression level 39 times higher than that in Thp cells.At the same time,mir-205 was also strongly induced in human Th2 cells in response to antigen stimulation, with a maximum induction at day 2 of differentiation.

Besides,miRNA-205 was not induced in human Th1 cells, indicating that its regulation is specific to Th2 cells.We also found that miR-205 was induced in human Th2 cells in response to the Th2-inducing cytokine thymic stromal lymphopoietin (TSLP), although to a lesser extent than in response to antigen,suggesting that both antigen-specific and cytokine-mediated signals are important for the induction of miR-205 in Th2 cells.

In a nut shall,miRNA-205 mediates the activity of type 2 helper T cells such as their activation,differentiation and migration,which gives us a better understanding of immune response and contributes to addressing metabolic challenges in CAR T cell therapy
